Bushfires of Australia

It's essential for Australians to understand the causes and impacts of Bushfires. This knowledge can enhance community resilience, ultimately reducing risks to life and property. Additionally, this knowledge fosters environmental awareness.

The following collection of visualisations displays various Bushfire information relevant to the average Australian. This includes recent Australian Bushfires, factors that may contribute to them, and the impact of such Bushfires.

This visualisation is a dot distribution map representing Australian Bushfires that occured in 2020. It displays their location and magnitude.

This Bushfire data was produced by NASA sattelites. The magnitude of these fires is derived from the brightness as seen from space.

Australian Bushfire Contributors

This visualisation depicts the average maximum temperature at numerous Australian locations from 1975 to 2019. The magnitude is represented by mark size and colour.

Large Temperatures contribute to bushfires by increasing evaporation, drying out vegetation, and enhancing the likelihood of ignition and the intensity of fires.

This visualisation depicts various environmental conditions that contribute to bushfires. The data was collected daily, at numerous Australian locations in 2016.

Rainfall, Humidity, and Windspeed contribute to bushfires by determining fuel moisture levels, the likelihood of ignition, and the rate at which fires can spread.

The impact of Australian Bushfires

During recent Australian Bushfires, millions of animals were killed and many more were displaced, leading to large declines in wildlife population.

Understanding the impact of Bushfires on wildlife is crucial for conservation efforts, informing recovery strategies, and fostering awareness.

This stacked bar chart represents the percentage of Australian forest-land damaged by Bushfires over the 2019-20 summer season.

Knowing the extent of forest land damaged by bushfires in Australia is vital for assessing environmental health and guiding reforestation efforts.

This dual-axis line chart depicts some very tragic data regarding Australia's large-scale Bushfires. This unfortunate information includes the number of human casualties, and land damaged by the fires.

The vertically concatenated line chart (beneath the larger bar chart) acts as a filter. To use this filter, drag your cursor to form a box, and then click and drag the box to focus the visualisation on areas of interest.

Many Australians have lost their lives in large-scale Bushfires. The knowledge of such losses is important when developing safety measures, and to honor the lives lost while fostering community resilience.

Knowing how much land was damaged in bushfires is vital for Australians to assess ecological consequences, guide recovery efforts, and implement better land management and fire prevention strategies.
